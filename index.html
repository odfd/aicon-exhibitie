AICON EXHIBITION 2023

AICON 2023

AI Art Exhibition

Art Exhibition focused on AI

AI is a rapidly evolving technology that only just recently is starting to impact society as a whole. This technology will change society as we know it. AICON aims to embrace this technology and make it conceptually accessible. To reach this goal AICON is designing thought provoking art installations in co-creation with cutting edge AI technology, residents of Rotterdam, artists and scientists.




Front-end (Room 1)

An art installation consisting of different artworks that have been created and curated by the AIcon team in collaboration with Artificial Intelligence. The starting point and inspiration of the artworks are self-portraits made by a diverse group of participants during our ‘Portraits of the City’ workshops we organized throughout the city of Rotterdam during the year 2022.

Each art piece is based on interpretations of human creativity combined with Artificial Intelligence. The digital outcomes are then translated into (abstract) physical objects involving craft and tactility. The exhibition shows a circular co-creation process in which we explore human creativity and Artificial Intelligence. The visitors of the exhibition (unknowingly) will also contribute to the installation as their feedback and comments will be directly translated into one of the artworks. Perhaps by passive audio recording that will translate in real-time to images in an ever growing mosaic grid projected on one of the walls.  .. All communication regarding this art installation (captions, exhibitions texts, promotion materials and press releases etc.) will be co-written by AI.




Back-end (Room 2)

A visual demonstration of the behind the scenes process. The ‘artist’ studio, a room where Artificial Intelligence is introduced as a co-creator of the art.  This consists of, for example, interactive displays of the audio recording process, from speech to text, processing the text, extracting keywords, prompt engineering, generating images/art, selecting from the generated images and ‘sending’ to the mosaic (the mosaic is projected in room 1).

Educational story diagrams about relevant AI topics, relevant to both the technology used in the exhibition, and relevant to the visitors. These diagrams will address thought provoking and sometimes controversial aspects of the selected topics, and shine light on the opportunities in which AI can be used to benefit each of us/the visitors as individuals, and society as a whole - as well as the social challenges. An optional feature would be an AI chatbot acting as if it is the artist answering all your questions regarding the exhibition or the project in general.

There might also be space allocated in this area for AICON’s journey; the process leading upto the exhibit: the NFT’s made with the participants of the workshops, photos/video of the workshops, Lucify implementation for the interactive diagrams or other augmented reality applications.
